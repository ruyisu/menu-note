# Menu Note Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 7, 2026

Menu Note is designed as a local-first macOS menu bar app for quick notes, links, and clipboard history.

## Data Collection

Menu Note does not collect, sell, share, or transmit personal data to the developer or to third parties.

The app does not require an account and does not use advertising, analytics, tracking SDKs, or cloud sync.

## Data Stored on Your Mac

Menu Note stores app data locally on your Mac, including:

- Notes and capture drafts
- Quick links
- Recent clipboard items
- App preferences

This data remains on your device unless you choose to copy, export, delete, or otherwise share it yourself.

## Clipboard Access

Menu Note can monitor clipboard content to provide clipboard history. Clipboard items are stored locally on your Mac and are not sent to the developer or any external server.

## Network Access

Menu Note does not require internet access for its core features. When you open a saved link, macOS will open that link in your default browser.

## Data Deletion

You can delete notes, links, and clipboard items within the app. You may also remove the app's local data from your Mac by deleting the app's storage folder.

## Third-Party Services

Menu Note does not integrate with third-party analytics, advertising, tracking, or cloud storage services.

## Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated if Menu Note's features or data practices change. Updates will be reflected by changing the effective date above.
